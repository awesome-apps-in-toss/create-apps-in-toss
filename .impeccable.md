## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are people in their 20s and 30s who are trying to build mini-apps.
They use the dashboard as an operational workspace to plan, scaffold, run, and review a mini-app launch pipeline.
The interface should reduce friction, keep the current step obvious, and help users move through the flow without confusion.

### Brand Personality
Simple, calm, practical.
The product should feel focused and reliable rather than promotional or playful.
It should support task completion with clear hierarchy and low visual noise.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a restrained, simple visual direction.
Avoid flashy, overly decorative, or high-intensity styles.
Prefer clear spacing, legible type, and understated emphasis over visual spectacle.

### Design Principles
1. Keep the next action obvious and easy to find.
2. Favor clarity and calm over decoration.
3. Use emphasis sparingly and only when it improves task flow.
4. Maintain a clean hierarchy that supports scanning and long-running workflows.
5. Avoid visually loud patterns that distract from operational status.
